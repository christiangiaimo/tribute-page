Jordan B. Peterson

Clinical psychologist and professor of psychology

Here's a time line of Jordan`s life:

  • 1962 - Born in Edmonton, Alberta
  • 1979 - Graduated from highschool
  • 1979- Entered Grande Prairie Regional College to study political science and English literature, studying to be a corporate lawyer.
  • 1982 - Transferred to the University of Alberta, where he completed his BA in political science in 1982.
  • 1985 - He moved to Montreal to attend McGill University to earn a PhD in clynical psichology
  • 1989 - Married his wife Tammy
  • 1991 - He earned his PhD in clinical psychology under the supervision of Robert O. Pihl
  • 1992 - His daughter Mikhaila was born.
  • 1993 - Peterson lived in Arlington, Massachusetts, while teaching and conducting research at Harvard University, where he was hired as an assistant professor in the psychology department.
  • 1998 - He returned to Canada in to become a full professor at the University of Toronto.
  • 1999 - His book Maps of Meaning: The Architecture of Belief was published - a work in which examined several academic fields to describe the structure of systems of beliefs and myths, their role in the regulation of emotion, creation of meaning, and motivation for genocide.
  • 1998 - He returned to Canada in to become a full professor at the University of Toronto.
  • 2004 - A 13-part TV series based on the book aired on TVOntario. He went on to have multiple appearences on that network throughout the years, on different shows.
  • 2013 - Started recording his lectures (Personality and Its Transformations & Maps of Meaning: The Architecture of Belief) and uploading them to YouTube.
  • 2015 - Along with colleagues Robert O. Pihl, Daniel Higgins, and Michaela Schippers, he launched a program with series of online writing exercises, titled the Self Authoring Suite.
  • 2016 - Started his own podcast, The Jordan B. Peterson Podcast.
  • 2017 - Hired a production team to film his psychology lectures at the University of Toronto. Along with colleagues Robert O. Pihl and Daniel Higgins, he launched an online personality assessment program for individuals, based on the Big Five Aspect Scale, titled Understand Myself. His granddaughter Elizabeth was born.
  • 2018 - His book 12 Rules for Life: An Antidote to Chaos was released.
  • 2021 - He retired from the University of Toronto, becoming professor emeritus.
  • 2022 - He became chancellor of the newly launched Ralston College, an unaccredited liberal arts education project.Along with Baroness Stroud and John Anderson, Peterson also founded the Alliance for Responsible Citizenship
  • 2024 - Peterson's fourth book, We Who Wrestle with God: Perceptions of the Divine, was published
